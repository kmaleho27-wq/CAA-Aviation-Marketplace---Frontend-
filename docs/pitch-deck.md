## Slide 3 — What Naluka does (3 bullets, in their order of pain)

1. **Document expiry watchdog.** Every licence, medical, type rating
   and AMP renewal is tracked. We notify your team 90, 30, and 7 days
   before expiry — and we notify the operator who manages them. Zero
   surprises.
2. **One-click audit pack.** Pick a date range, click export. PDF or
   JSON, with a hash-chained integrity proof every SACAA inspector or
   insurance underwriter can verify independently. The 3-week PDF hunt
   is a 3-second download.
3. **Escrow for crew + parts + MRO.** PayFast-backed escrow held until
   digital sign-off. Contractor knows the money's there. You know the
   work won't be invoiced before it's delivered. Audit trail recorded
   automatically.

## Slide 6 — How it works (operator's view)

1. **You sign up your team.** Either everyone self-registers, or upload
   a CSV — bulk import 200 pilots in 30 seconds.
2. **We verify each crew member** against their SACAA records, manually
   within 4 business hours. Once verified, they're listed and bookable.
3. **You hire a contractor or buy a part** through the marketplace.
   Money goes to PayFast escrow.
4. **Work happens.** When complete, the licensed signatory (Part 145
   engineer, pilot in command, DAME) signs off via biometric auth.
5. **Money releases automatically.** Audit chain entry written. Done.

This whole flow leaves a trail — every state change, every sign-off,
every release — that we can hand to a SACAA inspector tomorrow.

## Slide 10 — The ask

*"Would you pay R10,000/month, today, to replace your current
compliance setup with Naluka?"*

- **Yes, where do I sign?** → contract, onboarding call, ICP confirmed.
- **Yes, but…** → write down the "but". That's the next sprint.
- **No, here's why** → write down the why. That's product-market-fit
  data, often more valuable than a yes.

If you hear "no" 7 times in 10 calls and the "whys" cluster around
something we don't currently do — pivot. If they cluster around price,
it's a pricing problem. If they cluster around trust, it's a trust
signal problem (custom domain, ToS, customer references).

## Appendix — competitor positioning one-liners

- *"Won't CAMP/AMOS do this?"* — CAMP tracks airframes, not crew.
  AMOS tracks everything but you can't afford it.
- *"Won't SACAA build their own?"* — Maybe. They already did for
  pilots. Even if they do, you still need escrow and audit pack
  generation. We win when SACAA's free portal makes verification a
  commodity and our value moves up-stack.
- *"Why isn't this just a spreadsheet?"* — A spreadsheet doesn't
  notify you 90 days before expiry, doesn't generate audit packs in
  one click, doesn't hold funds in escrow, and doesn't survive a
  SACAA inspection.

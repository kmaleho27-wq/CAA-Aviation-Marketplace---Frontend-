# Tier 0 — Customer-development pack

This is the discovery pack, not the sales pack. The goal at Tier 0 is
**learning**, not selling. You're testing whether the problem we
designed the product around (multi-licence credential expiry +
escrow-trust gap on contractor work) is actually the top-three pain
for the people we think we're building for.

If 7+ out of 10 conversations confirm the problem, we keep building.
If under 5 confirm it, we pivot. Either outcome is a win — fast.

## The discovery email (NOT a sales email)

**Subject:** Quick research question — SACAA crew compliance

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'm researching how SA operators handle multi-licensed crew compliance
— specifically people who hold more than one SACAA credential
(ATPL + Cat B1, ATC + DAME, that kind of thing).

I'm not selling anything. I'm trying to validate whether the way
operators track licence + medical expiries today is actually painful,
or whether the spreadsheet works fine.

Could I borrow 25 minutes of your time on Zoom this or next week to
ask you about it? Happy to share what we hear back from other
operators in return — useful benchmarking either way.

Tuesday or Thursday morning would suit me. What works for you?

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
```

**Why this works:**
- Subject says "research" not "demo" — passes the BS filter
- Explicitly says "not selling" — disarming, makes them more likely to say yes
- Asks for 25 not 30 minutes — psychologically easier ceiling
- Offers reciprocity (benchmarking) — research-for-research trade
- One concrete ask (a meeting), no PDFs attached, no calendar links

## 25-minute interview script

Print this. Take notes by hand — not on a laptop. Eye contact
matters. Don't pitch unless they ask.

### Open (2 min)
> "Thanks for the time. Quick context: I'm building a tool that
> *might* help operators manage SACAA crew credentials, but before I
> over-invest, I want to learn how you actually do this today. I'll
> ask you about your process, then anything I'm missing. You don't
> have to look anything up — I just want your gut."

### Process (10 min) — open-ended, no leading
1. "Walk me through how you keep track of crew licences and medicals today."
2. "Who owns this? One person, a team, or distributed?"
3. "How do you know when something's about to expire?"
4. "Tell me about the last time someone showed up to a flight or shift with an expired credential — what happened?"
5. "Do you have crew with multiple SACAA licences (ATPL + B1 etc.)? How is that handled in your system?"

> *Listen. Don't interrupt. Don't pitch. Take quotes verbatim where you can.*

### Pain (8 min) — quantify the bleeding
6. "If you had to put a number on it, how much time per week does this consume across your team?"
7. "Has SACAA ever flagged you for an out-of-date doc on a crew member? What was the cost of fixing it?"
8. "Have you ever had to ground a flight or rotation because a credential lapsed unexpectedly? Walk me through that."
9. "On a scale of 0–10, how painful is this versus your other ops headaches? What ranks higher? What ranks lower?"

> *Question 9 is the gold. Anyone scoring 7+ is a buyer signal. Anyone
> scoring under 5 means we're solving a non-problem for them — log it
> and consider moving up-market or pivoting.*

### Solutions explored (3 min)
10. "Have you tried any tools or services to fix this? What happened?"
11. "What would the ideal solution look like? (You're not constrained — describe magic.)"

### Close (2 min)
- "What did I miss? Anything I should have asked but didn't?"
- "Mind if I follow up if I find someone else with a similar setup who solved this well?"
- "Can I send you what I learn from these conversations? (Reciprocity payoff.)"
- **Do NOT ask for a sale. Do NOT ask for a referral on this call.**

## Cadence

- Aim for **2 calls/week** for 5 weeks. Slow enough to sit with each.
- Send 5 outreach emails on Tuesday morning, 5 on Wednesday morning.
- Follow up after 5 days, then 8 days, then stop. Three touches max.
- After call 5, do an interim review — are you hearing the same thing?
- After call 10, decision point: **build more / pivot / kill**.

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't demo the product. They'll politely nod. Useless data.
- ❌ Don't ask "would you buy this?" Hypothetical buyers always say yes.
- ❌ Don't lead questions. "It's painful, right?" gives you the answer you want, not the truth.
- ❌ Don't fill silence. Wait. They'll say more interesting things if you let the pause breathe.
- ❌ Don't take notes on a laptop. Looks like you're emailing.
- ❌ Don't talk more than they do. If you're saying more than 30% of the words, you're failing.

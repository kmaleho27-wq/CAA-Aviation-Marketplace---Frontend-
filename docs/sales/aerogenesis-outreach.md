# Aerogenesis SA — partnership outreach email

The autoplan competitive map flagged Aerogenesis SA (and similar
SA aviation consultancies) as **partnership candidates, not
competitors**. They have the SACAA relationships and operator trust
we lack. We have the software they wish they had.

This is the first message. Goal: a 30-min Zoom to explore whether
a partnership shape works for both sides.

## The email

**To:** [Find a Director / MD / Head of Operations on their site
or LinkedIn — NOT info@aerogenesis or generic inboxes. The person
who'd actually decide on a partnership.]

**Subject:** Naluka × Aerogenesis SA — partnership exploration

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'll be brief — this is a partnership exploration, not a sales
pitch.

I've been building Naluka, a compliance and escrow platform
specifically for SA aviation operators. We do the software side:
crew expiry tracking, audit pack generation for SACAA inspections,
escrow on contractor and MRO payments. Live, working, but pre-
revenue.

Here's why I'm reaching out: the SA aviation operators I'm
targeting (5–30 aircraft fleets) already have a relationship with
someone like Aerogenesis for the compliance heavy lifting. You're
the trusted advisor; I'm the software they wish their advisor
had.

I see three possible shapes for a partnership:

1. **Referral.** Aerogenesis recommends Naluka to compliance
   clients, gets a margin share on subscriptions you originate.

2. **White-label.** Naluka under your brand, deeper integration
   with your existing service offering. We handle the platform,
   you handle the relationship.

3. **Co-sell.** Joint pitches to operators where the offer is
   "Aerogenesis advisory + Naluka platform" as one package.

I don't have a strong preference yet — I want to understand what
would make sense for your business first.

Would you have 30 minutes for a Zoom in the next week or two? I'd
walk you through what we've built and listen to where the friction
is in your current operator-side work. Worst case it's an hour you
already wanted to spend thinking about software in your sector.

[YOUR NAME]
[PHONE]
naluka.aero
```

## Why this works

- **Lead with "partnership not pitch"** — they get sold to all
  day. This stands out.
- **Acknowledge their position** ("trusted advisor") before
  proposing anything. Flatter strategically, not vapidly.
- **Offer three concrete shapes** — they can react to options,
  not invent them.
- **Don't pre-commit to a margin or structure** — that's the
  call.
- **No PDF attachment, no deck.** Save the deck for the call.

## What to bring to the call

- The deck, but only as a backup if they ask. Open with their
  business, not yours.
- A printed (or at least drafted) one-page partnership term sheet
  with three shapes mapped out. Hand it to them at minute 25 if
  the conversation goes well.
- A list of 3–5 specific operator names that would benefit from a
  joint pitch. Concrete > abstract.

## Realistic outcomes

| Probability | Outcome | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 30% | They love it, want to formalise quickly | Push for a paid pilot with one of their clients within 30 days |
| 30% | Polite interest, "let us think about it" | Follow up in 2 weeks. If still vague, they're just being polite. Move on. |
| 25% | They see Naluka as a threat, decline | Don't push. Note them as competitor not partner. Sell direct. |
| 15% | "Send us the deck" / no real engagement | Treat as no. Don't send the deck unless asked twice. |

If a partnership doesn't crystallise within 60 days, drop the
relationship and approach the operators directly. **Don't burn
months waiting on a partner.** They're optional, you don't need
them.

## Disqualifying signs in the first call

End the call early (politely) and move on if they:

- Ask you to share equity before any commercial structure exists
- Insist on exclusive arrangements before showing any pipeline
- Treat the conversation as a chance to recruit you to their team
- Say "we'll build it ourselves" — they won't, but they'll waste
  your time discussing it for 6 months

Good signals:

- They ask specific questions about how you handle [THEIR PAIN]
- They name customers they think it'd help — concrete operator
  names, not "the industry"
- They ask about commercial terms in the first 15 min
- They follow up themselves within 48 hours after the call

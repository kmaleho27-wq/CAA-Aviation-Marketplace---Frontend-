# Cold outreach email templates

Three templates for the three core ICPs. Use as starting points —
edit per recipient based on what you know about them. Goal of every
email: get a 30-min Zoom call, not to sell them anything in the email.

## Universal rules

- **Subject line ≤ 50 chars.** Mobile previews truncate long subjects.
- **Body ≤ 150 words.** If they don't read it, the email failed.
- **One ask only** — the call.
- **Personalise the first sentence** with something specific to
  them or their organisation. Generic openers ("I came across your
  business…") get deleted.
- **Send Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday morning, 8–10 AM SAST.**
  Mondays are too busy, Fridays too checked-out.
- **Follow up after 5 days** if no reply. Then once more after 8 days.
  Then stop — three touches max.

## Template 1 — Operator compliance officer

**Use when:** the recipient is responsible for crew compliance at a
small/mid airline (5–30 aircraft, charter or scheduled). Likely
title: "Compliance Officer", "Quality Manager", "Director of
Operations".

**Subject:** Quick compliance question for [AIRLINE]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I noticed [SOMETHING SPECIFIC — recent fleet expansion, route
launch, SACAA mention, LinkedIn post about a hire]. Congratulations.

I'm building Naluka — a compliance OS for SA aviation operators
that automates the things your team currently does in spreadsheets:
crew expiry tracking, audit pack generation for SACAA inspections,
escrow on contractor payments.

Two questions I'd love your read on:

1. How much of your last SACAA inspection prep was PDF hunting?
2. What's the worst near-miss you've had with a lapsed licence?

If those resonate, would you have 30 minutes for a Zoom call this
week or next? I'd show you the platform end-to-end and ask for
your honest take. No pitch deck, no follow-up emails — just
research for me, hopefully useful framing for you.

[YOUR NAME]
[PHONE]
naluka.aero
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific opener proves it's not a mass mailing.
- Two questions reframe you from seller to researcher.
- The "no pitch deck, no follow-up" line lowers their reply cost.
- Phone number shows you're real.

## Template 2 — AMO operations manager

**Use when:** the recipient runs operations at a SACAA Part 145 AMO.
Likely title: "Operations Manager", "Maintenance Manager", "Quality
Manager", or "Accountable Manager".

**Subject:** Naluka — a question about contractor payments

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

[SPECIFIC OPENER — saw their AMO certificate post, recent Part 145
expansion, a hire announcement, etc.]

Quick context: I'm building Naluka, a platform that handles the
software side of running a Part 145 AMO — contractor onboarding,
8130/RTS sign-off workflow, escrow on customer payments before
release-to-service.

The reason I'm reaching out: I want to understand the real friction
of how you currently get paid. Specifically:

1. How long after work-complete do you typically wait to be paid?
2. What's your worst write-off in the last 12 months?

If escrow on day one of work would change that calculus, I'd love
30 minutes of your time to walk through how Naluka does it. Tuesday
or Thursday morning work?

[YOUR NAME]
[PHONE]
```

**Why this works:**
- Naluka's MRO escrow is a real differentiator vs how AMOs usually
  get paid (30-90 days net). Lead with the financial pain.
- "How much do you write off" is a question their CFO is also
  asking. Aligns the OPS manager with internal pressure.

## Template 3 — Aviation contractor (Part 66 engineer, ATPL pilot, etc.)

**Use when:** the recipient is a licenced aviation professional who
takes contract work — AMEs, contract pilots, DAMEs running their
own practice, etc.

**Subject:** Question for a [PART 66 / ATPL / DAME / etc.]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I came across your work at [SPECIFIC CONTEXT — LinkedIn,
forum post, mutual contact recommendation, etc.].

I'm building Naluka — think of it as the equivalent of Upwork or
Toptal for SACAA-licenced aviation professionals. Listings are
verified by us before you appear, money goes into escrow before
you start work, payment hits when you sign off.

Two questions I'd love your take on:

1. How do operators currently find you when they need a contract
   [Part 66 engineer / pilot / DAME]?
2. How often do you do work and chase payment for 30+ days after?

If either of those is a real pain, I'd love 20 minutes to walk
you through how Naluka would change it. We're free for individuals
— 3% on completed contracts, no subscription.

[YOUR NAME]
[PHONE]
```

**Why this works:**
- Two pains: (a) finding work, (b) getting paid for it. One of those
  always resonates.
- "Free for individuals" defuses the "what's the catch" reflex.
- 20 min instead of 30 — lower commitment ask matches the
  contractor's lower commitment level vs an enterprise buyer.

## Tracking

Set up a simple sheet:

| Recipient | Org | Role | Sent | Reply? | Call set? | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Track:
- Reply rate (target ≥ 15% on cold list)
- Call-set rate (target ≥ 30% of replies)
- "Yes I'd pay" rate (target ≥ 30% of calls = 7/10 calls means PMF)

If reply rate is < 5%, the templates aren't working — try a
different opener or shorter body. If reply rate is ≥ 15% but
call-set rate is < 10%, there's a calendar friction problem (use
Calendly).
